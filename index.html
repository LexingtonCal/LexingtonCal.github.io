Standard Poodles

By Isabelle

Introduction To Standard Poodles

Standard Poodles are very athletic and are one of the Smartest Dogs Breeds in the world. They are originally from Germany and the name Poodle comes from the German Word, Pudelin. This means to splash in the water. These beautiful dogs can come in many sizes and colours.

The 3 Poodle Sizes Are...

  • toy (small)
  • miniature (medium)
  • standard (large)

The Most Common Poodle Colours Are...

  • Black
  • Brown
  • Red
  • White

Poodle Temperament

Poodles are a very friendly dog-breed. Although Standard Poodles can sometimes be very large, they are very gentle and great around kids and other dogs. Poodles are very easy to train and need lots of exercise to remain calm. These dogs love attention so they don’t do well being left alone for long periods of time. Poodles love to be around lots of people and other dogs. They are very easy to train and are extremely loyal to their owners. Poodles are often stereotyped as diva dogs, but they are very faithful to their owners and are great guard dogs.

How Much Exercise Standard Poodles Need

Standard Poodles need a minimum of 30-40 minutes of exercise per day, but 1 hour is highly recommended. Poodles love a good game of fetch, or even just a nice walk. Poodles love to be active and they enjoy playing with humans and other dogs.

Standard Poodle Characteristics

Poodles have beautiful curly hair (not fur). They come in many different colors. Standard Poodles have quite long legs and are a bit muscly. These gorgeous pooches also have long snouts and cute floppy ears. Another beautiful feature to these kinds of dogs is their long fluffy tail.

Poodle Grooming

Poodles have fluffy, curly fur that can be easily matted. In order to maintain their beautiful coat, they need almost daily brushing and a haircut every 3-6 weeks is highly recommended if you want to keep your poodle looking fresh. Getting your poodles’ hair trimmed can be quite expensive but if you brush them and wash them regularly, your poodle might not need to be cut too often. Poodles should be bathed every 3 weeks. If you don’t want to do this at home, then you can always get it professionally done. Getting your poodles’ hair cut can depend on how often it is trimmed and what style you want.
